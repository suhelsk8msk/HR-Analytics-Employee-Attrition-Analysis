# **HR Analytics Employee Attrition Analysis**

## Project Overview

Employee attrition is one of the most critical challenges faced by organizations. High employee turnover can increase recruitment costs, reduce productivity, and impact overall business performance.

This project analyzes employee attrition data using **Python, SQL, and Power BI** to identify the key factors influencing employee turnover and provide data-driven recommendations for improving employee retention.

The analysis covers employee demographics, compensation, job satisfaction, work-life balance, overtime, business travel, departmental trends, and other HR-related factors that contribute to attrition.

---

## Project Objectives

- Analyze employee attrition patterns.
- Identify key factors contributing to employee turnover.
- Perform data cleaning and exploratory data analysis.
- Write SQL queries to extract business insights.
- Build an interactive Power BI dashboard.
- Generate actionable HR recommendations.

# Key Business Insights

## Employee Attrition

- Overall attrition rate is approximately **16.12%**.
- Employee turnover remains a significant HR concern.

## Overtime Impact

- Employees working overtime exhibit noticeably higher attrition rates.
- Overtime appears to be one of the strongest indicators of employee turnover.

## Department Analysis

- The Sales department contributes the highest number of employee exits.
- HR and management should focus retention efforts on high-turnover departments.

## Job Role Analysis

- Certain job roles experience significantly higher attrition than others.
- High-risk roles should be prioritized for employee engagement initiatives.

## Salary Analysis

- Employees in lower income groups are more likely to leave the organization.
- Compensation and reward strategies may require review.

## Age Group Analysis

- Younger employees demonstrate higher attrition rates compared to experienced employees.
- Early-career retention programs may help reduce turnover.

## Job Satisfaction

- Lower job satisfaction levels are associated with increased attrition.
- Employee engagement programs can improve retention.

## Work-Life Balance

- Employees reporting poor work-life balance are more likely to leave.
- Workload and flexibility policies should be reviewed.

## Promotion Analysis

- Employees with longer durations since their last promotion show higher attrition tendencies.
- Career growth opportunities are critical for employee retention.

## Business Travel

- Frequent business travel may contribute to employee dissatisfaction and turnover.
- Travel-intensive roles may require additional support mechanisms.

---

# Business Recommendations

### Reduce Overtime Dependency

- Monitor excessive overtime.
- Improve workforce planning.
- Balance workloads effectively.

### Improve Employee Compensation

- Review compensation structures for lower salary groups.
- Introduce competitive salary benchmarking.

### Strengthen Career Growth Opportunities

- Provide clear promotion pathways.
- Establish employee development programs.

### Enhance Employee Engagement

- Conduct regular employee satisfaction surveys.
- Implement recognition and reward programs.

### Improve Work-Life Balance

- Introduce flexible work policies where applicable.
- Encourage healthy workload distribution.

### Focus on High-Risk Departments

- Conduct detailed attrition investigations within departments exhibiting high turnover.
- Develop department-specific retention strategies.
